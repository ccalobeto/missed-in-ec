TYPOLOGY

87% of disappearances are voluntary according to the cataloging established in the Protocol of Action of the Security Forces and Bodies in Cases of Missing Persons. The 6.6% are disappearances with signs of criminality, which is a very high rate compared to Spain (p. 40) which is bellow 1%.

CATEGORY

Segregating the Typology, it is observed that the Family Problems category is the cause of more than 50% of the disappearances.

SEX

More than 58% of the disappearances registered in 2023 are women.

AGE GROUPS

44% of the registered complaints correspond to minors between 13 and 17 years old. People over 65 years of age represent 4.4% of cases.

CANTONS OF DISAPPEARANCE CASES PER 10,000 INHABITANTS

Quito (1,364), Guayaquil (782) and Santo Domingo de los Tsahilas (224) are the Cantons with the highest number of cases of missing persons. Taking into account the size of the population, it is observed that in most cantons there are less than 7 cases per 10,000 inhabitants; unlike Paquisha and Morona, which are the places where the most disappearances occur with 12; followed by Sucua, El Pangui and Alausi with 10 and 9 cases.
